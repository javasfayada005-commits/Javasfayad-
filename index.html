Gajah Mungkur Reservoir is the largest reservoir in Central Java, located in Wonogiri, and serves as a multipurpose dam to stem the flow of the Bengawan Solo River. Inaugurated in 1981, this reservoir plays a vital role as a flood control facility, irrigation facility for tens of thousands of hectares of rice fields, and energy source through hydroelectric power plants, although its construction initially required thousands of families to transmigrate as dozens of villages were submerged. Now, beyond its technical function, Gajah Mungkur Reservoir has developed into a leading tourist destination offering water rides, freshwater fish cuisine, and a modern glass bridge, making it an important pillar of food security and the tourism economy in the region.

About Us

Make Your Tour Memorable and Safe With Us

Deep in the southern region of Central Java, specifically in Wonogiri Regency, lies the majestic and serene Gajah Mungkur Reservoir. Surrounded by green hills, this reservoir serves as a lifeline for the surrounding community. Its expansive waters serve as flood control, irrigation, power generation, and a tourist destination blending with the natural beauty and activities of local fishermen.
